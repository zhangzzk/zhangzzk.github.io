My Research Profile

After the Big Bang when the universe begins to cool down, the large-scale structure of dark matter comes into being and the first galaxies are believed to form and ionize the cosmos. It is known as the Cosmic Dawn and Reionization (CD/EoR). Currntly to probe such early universe faces great challenges since it's so far away in the redshift space and therefore we have extremely weak signals. Fortunately, we expect the next-generation experiments such as SKA and JWST to shed light on the visible universe in its very infancy. At the moment, I am a member of the SKA group in Shanghai Astronomical Observatory. My interest focuses on the property of the first sources, for example galaxies and supermassive black holes, and how they reflect the nature of dark matter and interact with the medium pervading the space.

I'm also excited about measuring the cosmic structure by taking advantages of gravitational lensing effects, especially weak lensing. I am a member of the cosmic shear measurement group for the up-coming Chinese Space Station Telescope (CSST) and deeply invoved in building an end-to-end pipeline. My part focuses on exctracting the shear signal using Metacalibration technique and I'm also developing a new galaxies' shape measurement method with Machine Learning.

Generally speaking, my interests cover a variety of topics in galaxies and cosmology. I have a great passion for the true nature of our cosmos and potentials for further academic career. I'm very excited to learn new things and probably a fast learner. Here you can find my CV.
